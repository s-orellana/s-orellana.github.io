PhD work

In my first project I examined how immuno-metabolic health changes associated with early life stress are linked to differences in brain structure in adulthood. Brain and body are often examined separately, despite ample evidence of their mutual influence. I believe that properly describing these interactions can teach us something about how psychiatric illness is developed and sustained, and how we may effectively treat it.

In my second project I described how different brain regions are synchronised in their structural change across adolescence. Some regions primarily orchestrate sensory and motor processes (seeing, riding a bike), others orchestrate more abstract thought (thinking about others, planning dinner). If we understand how these are jointly changing after puberty, then we can better understand the unique emotional and social changes we observe in adolescents.
